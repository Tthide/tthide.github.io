Why UX?

Ever since I was a kid, I always knew I wanted to create and design things, especially if it could help people in their daily life. I was (and still am) very curious about science and how things work. I would draw mechanisms that popped in my head, build pipes out of paper sheets to create circuits for marbles or be fascinated not by knights but rather by how trebuchets and drawbridges work. It's all of this that made me want to become an engineer.

Discovering UX principles on a random Instagram post was a game-changer for me. Indeed, I have always thought of designing and creating things for the sake of efficiency (a very engineer mindset indeed). However, I never really considered how people could be impacted by designs and that, ultimately, it is people that use and should benefit from the things we create. That’s when it clicked:

Engineering shouldn’t just be about making systems work; it should also be about making them enjoyable, intuitive, and meaningful for the people who use them.

And now I combine these two schools of thought in my approach to design experiences: Efficiency and Human-Centeredness.
